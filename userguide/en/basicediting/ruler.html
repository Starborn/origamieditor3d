Ruler

The Ruler is the default tool for editing an origami. It appears in the 3D View as a red line. It is also displayed on the Crease Pattern, revealing the folding lines corresponding to the Ruler's current position.

The Ruler can be activated by right clicking on the 3D View, and it can be fixed by moving the mouse somewhere else and right clicking again. After that, a small menu will pop up that looks like this:

What the Rotate and Cut commands do may be fairly obvious, but the Reflect command is much more complicated than it may seem. It is explained in this chapter in detail.

The correct usage of the Ruler boils down to the following steps:

  1. Decide what lines you want to crease along.
  2. If necessary, use dragging in the 3D View to rotate the model into the correct orientation. A correct orientation is where all the new folding lines you want to add seem to line up perfectly into one straight line.
  3. Right click on a point you want the folding line to go through.
  4. Move the cursor around and decide where to set the Ruler.
  5. Take a look at the red lines in the Crease Pattern view and check if the folding lines you wanted are really there.
  6. Right click again to fix the Ruler in its final position.

The precision of the Ruler is enhanced by the Snap options. By default, the Ruler will be snapped to all corners of the paper, every vertex and every midpoint.

The Ruler can be deactivated by left clicking on the 3D View.
